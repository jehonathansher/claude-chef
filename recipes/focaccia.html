Focaccia — 90% Hydration

One 30×40 cm tray · ~3–4 cm thick · same-day · ready 13:30

Airy, thick focaccia with an open crumb and crisp crust. Tuned for Shtibel artisan flour (11.4% protein) in a climate-controlled Emek Hefer kitchen. Mixer knead, same-day ferment, timed from 07:30 to serving at 13:30.

Ingredients

gShtibel artisan flour (11.4% protein)
mlWarm water (start with 820, hold 80 back)
gInstant dry yeast
gSalt
mlOlive oil (dough + tray + finish)
gCoarse salt, to scatter
Fresh rosemary sprigs (optional)
Scale:

Steps

  1. Mix & autolyse. ~07:30 · In a mixer with dough hook, combine 1000g flour, 820ml of the warm water, and 12g yeast on low ~2 min, just until no dry flour remains. Cover, rest 25 min. With low-protein flour this is critical — the rest builds gluten on its own.
  2. Knead. Add 20g salt and 30ml of the olive oil. Knead at medium 8–9 min until the dough wraps the hook and turns glossy. Don't overwork — weak gluten can break. If it holds well, add the reserved 80ml water gradually. It stays sticky and loose — that's correct.
  3. Bulk + folds. ~07:55–10:20 · Move to a lightly oiled bowl, in a calm spot away from the AC airflow. Do 4 sets of stretch-and-folds, 30 min apart. At 23–24°C the whole ferment takes ~2–2.5 h — wait for 50–70% rise and big bubbles. Go by look.
  4. Into the tray. ~10:20 · Generously oil a 30×40 cm tray with the remaining olive oil. Tip in all the dough, fold over itself once or twice, gently stretch toward the corners without knocking out air. If it shrinks back, rest 10 min and try again.
  5. Proof in tray. ~10:30–12:15 · Cover and proof (away from the AC) until puffed, bubbly, and slow to spring back — about 1.5–2 h. When a gentle press leaves a dent that fills slowly, it's ready.
  6. Heat oven. ~12:05 · Heat to 230°C static (or 215°C fan) for at least 30 min. A baking stone/steel on the bottom helps even heat.
  7. Dimple & top. ~12:35 · Oil your fingertips and press deep to the tray bottom for the classic dimples, but gently. Scatter 6g coarse salt, add 6 rosemary sprigs if using, and drizzle more olive oil to fill the dimples.
  8. Bake. ~12:40 · 25–30 min to a deep golden-amber. Thick dough needs a touch longer — make sure the center is baked through, not damp. Pale sides? Give it 2–3 min more.
  9. Cool. Out of the tray immediately onto a rack so the bottom doesn't steam. Cool at least 20 min before slicing — cutting hot wrecks the texture. Ready to serve at 13:30.
No rosemary → leave plain or use thyme. Kitchen cooler than 22°C → use 14g yeast.

Notes

1. Flour. At 11.4% protein the gluten is weaker, so 90% hydration is its upper edge. Start at 820ml; add the reserve only if the dough can take it. If it breaks down and stays liquid, stop at 87–88% — workable dough beats wet dough.

2. Autolyse + folds are your friends — they build structure without aggressive kneading that could snap weak gluten.

3. AC. Keep the kitchen ~23–24°C, but never put the bowl or tray under the vent — direct airflow dries the surface and hurts the proof.

4. Open crumb. Don't knock out air when transferring to the tray, and don't over-dimple before baking.

5. Thickness. In a 30×40 tray, 1kg flour gives ~3–4 cm thick focaccia. Want thinner → split into two small trays. Extra thick → use a 25×33 tray (add 4–6 min bake, check the center well).

Pro tipGo by look, not the clock. The schedule assumes 23–24°C — if your kitchen runs warmer or cooler, the dough moves faster or slower. Trust the 50–70% rise and the slow-filling dimple.
