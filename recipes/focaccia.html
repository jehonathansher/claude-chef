Focaccia — 90% Hydration

One 30×40 cm tray · ~3–4 cm thick · ~6 h same-day · Shtibel flour (11.4%)

Ingredients

gShtibel artisan flour (11.4% protein)
mlWarm water
gInstant dry yeast
gSalt
mlOlive oil (dough + tray + finish)
gCoarse salt, to scatter
Fresh rosemary sprigs (optional)
×cm — rescales everything by area
Quick scale:

Steps

  1. Mix. Hook: 1000g flour + 820ml water + 12g yeast, low 2 min to no dry flour. Rest 25 min.
  2. Knead. Add 20g salt + 30ml oil. Medium 8–9 min till glossy, wrapping hook. Dribble in reserved 80ml water. Sticky is right.
  3. Bulk + folds. 4 stretch-and-folds, 30 min apart. Done at 50–70% rise, big bubbles (~2–2.5 h).
  4. Tray. Oil a 30×40 tray well. Tip in, stretch to corners. Don't deflate.
  5. Proof. Covered, ~1.5–2 h. Ready when a press dents and fills slowly.
  6. Oven. 230°C static / 215°C fan, 30 min min.
  7. Dimple. Oiled fingers to the bottom. Scatter 6g coarse salt + 6 rosemary sprigs, drizzle oil into dimples.
  8. Bake. 25–30 min to deep golden-amber. Center must be dry.
  9. Cool. Onto a rack. 20 min min before slicing.
No rosemary → plain or thyme. Kitchen under 22°C → 14g yeast.
Notes

Flour. 11.4% protein = weaker gluten, so 90% is the upper edge. Start at 820ml; add the reserve only if the dough takes it. Breaking down / staying liquid → stop at 87–88%.

Build structure with the rest + folds, not aggressive kneading that could snap weak gluten.

AC. Keep ~23–24°C, but never under the vent — airflow dries the surface and hurts the proof.

Open crumb. Don't knock out air into the tray, don't over-dimple.

Thickness. 30×40 + 1kg flour = ~3–4 cm. Thinner → two small trays. Thicker → 25×33 tray (+4–6 min bake).

Pro tipGo by look, not the clock. The schedule assumes 23–24°C — if your kitchen runs warmer or cooler, the dough moves faster or slower. Trust the 50–70% rise and the slow-filling dimple.
