Carbonara

Time ~25 min
Serves 2
Level Easy
Sources Italia Squisita · InRome Cooking · La Cucina Italiana
Ingredients
Spaghetti 200g
Guanciale 150g
Egg yolks 3
Whole egg 1
Pecorino Romano, grated 60g
Black pepper, cracked generous
Salt for water
Substitutions

No guanciale? Pancetta or thick-cut bacon.

No Pecorino? 30g Pecorino + 30g Parmigiano.

No cream — ever.

1
Render the guanciale
Cut 150g guanciale into thick lardons. Cold pan, medium heat. Cook until fat turns glass-clear and edges are crispy. Turn off heat — leave everything in the pan.
2
Make the sauce
Whisk 3 yolks + 1 whole egg + 60g Pecorino with a generous amount of cracked black pepper into a smooth paste. Monosilio's ratio: 1 yolk per person + 1 whole egg for the batch.
3
Cook the pasta
Boil 200g spaghetti in well-salted water. Pull it out 2 min early — it finishes in the pan.
4
Save the pasta water
Before draining, scoop out a full mug (~200g) of pasta water. This is your emulsifier — don't skip it.
5
Combine pasta + guanciale
Toss drained pasta into the guanciale pan (heat off). Add ~30g pasta water, toss until glossy and absorbed.
6
Let it cool slightly
Wait 30 seconds. The pan must be warm — not hot. Too hot = scrambled eggs.
7
Add the egg mixture
Pour the egg paste over the pasta off the heat. Toss constantly. Add pasta water ~20g at a time until the sauce coats every strand — creamy, not dry, not runny.
8
Plate immediately
Serve right away. Crack more black pepper on top. Nothing else needed.
Pro tip

The #1 Roman rule — eggs go in off the heat, always. The pasta's warmth does the cooking, not the pan. Pasta water is what makes it silky: add slowly, keep tossing.
