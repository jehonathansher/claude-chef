Carbonara

Roman · serves 2 · ~25 min
Sources: Italia Squisita, InRome Cooking, La Cucina Italiana

Ingredients

gSpaghetti
gGuanciale
Egg yolks
Whole egg
gPecorino Romano, grated
Black pepper · Salt for water
Scale:

Steps

  1. Render guanciale. Cut 150g guanciale into lardons. Cold pan, medium heat. Cook until fat is glass-clear, edges crisp. Heat off, leave in pan.
  2. Sauce. Whisk 3 yolks + 1 whole egg + 60g Pecorino + lots of black pepper to a paste.
  3. Pasta. Boil 200g spaghetti in salted water. Pull 2 min early.
  4. Save water. Scoop a mug (~200g) of pasta water before draining.
  5. Combine. Pasta into pan (heat off). Add ~30g pasta water, toss until glossy.
  6. Cool 30s. Pan warm, not hot. Too hot = scrambled eggs.
  7. Add egg. Pour egg paste off heat, toss constantly. Add water ~20g at a time until it coats every strand — creamy, not runny.
  8. Plate. Serve now, crack more pepper. Done.
No guanciale → pancetta/bacon. No Pecorino → half Parmigiano. No cream, ever.
Pro tipEggs always go in off the heat — the pasta's warmth cooks them, not the pan. Pasta water makes it silky: add slow, keep tossing.
